Rethinking Reasoning in Remote Sensing

Abstract

Recent multimodal reasoning models, inspired by DeepSeek-R1, have significantly advanced visionâ€“language systems. However, in remote sensing (RS) tasks, we observe widespread pseudo reasoning: models narrate the process of reasoning rather than genuinely reason toward the correct answer based on visual evidence. We attribute this to the Glance Effect, where a single, coarse perception of large-scale RS imagery results in incomplete understanding and reasoning based on linguistic self-consistency instead of visual evidence.

To address this, we propose RS-EoT (Remote Sensing Evidence-of-Thought), a language-driven, iterative visual evidence-seeking paradigm. To instill this paradigm, we propose SocraticAgent, a self-play multi-agent system that synthesizes reasoning traces via alternating cycles of reasoning and visual inspection. To enhance and generalize these patterns, we propose a two-stage progressive RL strategy: first, RL on fine-grained Grounding tasks to enhance RS-EoT capabilities, followed by RL on RS VQA to generalize to broader understanding scenarios. Experiments show RS-EoT achieves state-of-the-art performance on multiple RS VQA and grounding benchmarks. Analyses reveal clear iterative cycles of reasoning and evidence seeking, confirming RS-EoT mitigates the Glance Effect and enables genuine evidence-grounded reasoning.

Methodology: Asking like Socrates

1. SocraticAgent

Inspired by the Socratic Methodâ€”which fosters knowledge acquisition through a structured process of questioning rather than direct instructionâ€”we designed SocraticAgent.

It is a self-play multi-agent system comprising a Reasoner (pure-text logic) and a Perceiver (visual interface). They engage in a multi-turn dialogue:

  • The Reasoner poses incremental perceptual questions.
  • The Perceiver provides grounded visual answers.
  • A self-play prompting mechanism ensures detailed, step-by-step reasoning traces without "jumping" to conclusions.

2. Two-Stage Progressive RL

To enhance and generalize the RS-EoT capability, we employ a progressive Reinforcement Learning pipeline:

Stage 1
RL-Grounding ("Iron Sharpens Iron"):
We first train on fine-grained grounding tasks. Since these tasks demand precise visual evidence, they specifically strengthen the iterative evidence-seeking behavior.
Stage 2
RL-VQA (Generalization):
We then generalize to broader VQA scenarios. To prevent reward hacking on simple datasets, we introduce a novel multiple-choice reconstruction strategy with graded rewards.
